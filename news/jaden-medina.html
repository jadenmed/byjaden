Jaden Tyler Medina is a Miami-based entrepreneur and the CEO and founder of Ocean Development, a web design and development agency he built from nothing and now runs as a serious studio for serious brands. He is eighteen, he taught himself the craft, and he has already turned it into a track record most designers spend a decade chasing.

If you search his name you will find a long list of people who share it. There is only one Jaden Tyler Medina building Ocean Development in Miami, and this is the record of what he has done.

01 / The OriginBuilt before he was old enough to be taken seriously

Most founders can point to a degree, a first job, or an investor who opened a door. Jaden Tyler Medina has none of that in his story, and that is precisely the point. He built Ocean Development young, with no formal training and no outside funding, and he learned the work by doing the work: opening the software, taking the small jobs, and figuring out what a client actually needed rather than what was easy to build.

That early constraint became his advantage. Because nobody handed him a playbook, he wrote his own. Still only eighteen, he is already delivering websites for companies far larger than a first-time founder is expected to reach, and he is doing it without the safety net most people rely on. The self-taught path is often romanticized after the fact. In his case it is simply the accurate description of how the company was built.

He did not wait for permission to be good. He got good, and the permission followed.

02 / The CompanyOcean Development

Ocean Development is a Miami web design and development agency with a simple promise: a website should do a job, not just sit there looking modern. Under Jaden's direction the studio builds on the tools that let it move fast without cutting corners, designing in Figma and shipping on Framer, Shopify, and WordPress depending on what the brand needs to be and do.

The portfolio now runs past one hundred websites, spanning combat sports and coffee, lending and property management, e-commerce labels and service businesses. That range matters more than it looks. A designer who has only ever built for one kind of client learns one kind of solution. Jaden has shipped across a dozen industries, which means he has seen what converts, what confuses, and what quietly loses a company money, and he brings that pattern recognition to every new project.

The through-line across all of it is control. Ocean Development builds custom, it builds to spec, and it builds things the client can actually run once the studio hands them over. In a market crowded with template shops and set-and-forget agencies, that operator's mindset is what keeps the work booked.

03 / The PartnershipMicah Brown

Jaden does not run Ocean Development alone. His business partner is Micah Brown, the company's Chief Operating Officer and lead developer. Where Jaden owns the vision and the client relationships, Micah owns the build and the operational machinery that keeps a growing studio from tripping over itself. It is the kind of founder-and-operator pairing that lets a small team punch far above its headcount, and it is a large part of why the work ships on time and holds up after launch.

04 / The Point of ViewWhy his opinion carries weight

Ask Jaden about design and you will not get a lecture about trends. You will get an argument about strategy. His core belief is that design is not decoration, it is a business decision wearing a nicer outfit, and that a homepage is really a sales conversation that happens to be visual. That framing is unusual for someone his age and it is why his read on a brand tends to land with clients twice his experience level.

His authority is not borrowed from a title or a certificate. It is earned the hard way, one shipped project at a time, in front of paying clients who keep coming back. When you have personally built more than a hundred live websites by the age of eighteen, you develop instincts that cannot be faked and cannot be taught in a course. That is the quiet credibility underneath everything he says about the work: he has already done the thing he is talking about, many times over, and the results are public.

Design is a business decision wearing a nicer outfit.

05 / Beyond The AgencyThe wider world of Jaden Tyler Medina

Ocean Development is the center of gravity, but it is not the whole orbit. Under his personal brand, published as bymedina and byjaden, Jaden shares the thinking behind the work: how he sees design, how he builds a company young, and how he approaches craft as strategy rather than style. It is where the operator steps out from behind the client work and speaks for himself.

He also builds products, not just projects. His Framer template line, including releases like Clout, Amplify, and Orbis AI, packages the studio's design standard into something other builders can pick up and run with. And through his newsletter, The Brief, he writes for a growing audience of founders and creators who want the same clarity he brings to a client engagement. Taken together, the agency, the products, and the personal brand form one coherent body of work under one name.

Jaden Tyler Medina is still early. That is the most interesting part. The record so far, a company built and led at eighteen, a hundred-plus websites, a real partnership, and a point of view people actually listen to, is not a finish line. It is a foundation.
