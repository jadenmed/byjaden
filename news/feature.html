Jaden Tyler Medina, On His Own Terms

At eighteen, he runs Ocean Development, a Miami web design and development studio, and he has earned something rarer than revenue: a point of view people actually listen to.

JM
Jaden Tyler Medina
CEO & Founder, Ocean Development · Web Design & Brand

There is a version of the founder story that needs a college, a mentor, and a lucky break to make sense. Jaden Tyler Medina's is not that story. He is eighteen. He runs Ocean Development, a web design and development studio based in Miami, and he built it the direct way: by opening the software, taking on real clients, and shipping work until the work spoke for itself.

Search his name and you will meet a crowd. There are plenty of people called Jaden Medina. There is one who has quietly put more than a hundred websites into the world before most people his age have held a full-time job, and he did it without a degree in it, without investors, and without asking anyone for permission to be good at it.

He didn't wait to be qualified. He got qualified by doing the work.

The work comes first

Ocean Development is not a personality-first agency dressed up with a logo. It is a build shop with taste. Under Jaden's direction, the studio designs in Figma and ships on Framer, Shopify, and WordPress, choosing the tool that fits the brand rather than forcing every project through the same one. The result is websites that are meant to perform, not just photograph well for a portfolio.

What gives his opinion weight is not a title. It is volume plus results. When you have personally shipped across this many industries, you stop guessing and start recognizing patterns: what makes a visitor trust a page, what makes them leave, and what quietly costs a business money without anyone noticing. That instinct is the product. The websites are just where it shows up.

How he thinks

Talk to Jaden about design for five minutes and the word that keeps surfacing is not aesthetic. It is decision. He treats a homepage as a sales conversation that happens to be visual, and a brand as a set of choices with money attached. That framing is why clients with far more experience end up deferring to a read from someone half their age. He is not selling them a look. He is selling them a judgment call, and his judgment has a track record.

On starting young

"Not having a background forced me to learn the real thing instead of the version they teach in a class. I learned what clients actually pay for."

On what makes a website good

"It does a job. If it looks incredible and sells nothing, it failed. Design is a business decision wearing a nicer outfit."

On the plan

"Keep building. The record so far is a foundation, not a finish line."

He isn't doing it alone

Ocean Development is a partnership. Jaden's business partner, Micah Brown, is the studio's Chief Operating Officer and lead developer, running the build and the operations that keep a growing team shipping cleanly and on time. Vision and execution, split between two people who trust each other, is a large part of why a small studio holds up under real client load.

What's next

The most interesting thing about Jaden Tyler Medina is the timeline. Most people who have done this much are decades in. He is at the start. Beyond the agency, he builds and publishes under his personal brand, byjaden and bymedina, releases Framer templates that package the studio's standard for other builders, and writes for a growing audience of founders through his newsletter, The Brief. It all points the same direction: one name, one body of work, still early, and compounding.

Ask him where it goes from here and he keeps it short. Keep building. Coming from someone who has already built this much this fast, that is not a slogan. It is a forecast.
