What's actually working in web design right now.

A look at the trends, tools, and teardowns that are moving the needle for brands in 2026 — and the ones that are dead on arrival.

JM
Jaden Medina
Founder, Ocean Development

Every week I look at dozens of new sites, teardown what's working, and pass along the lessons. This week: three shifts in web design that are actually changing how brands convert visitors into customers.

1. Motion is replacing copy

The best-performing sites I've reviewed this month lean on subtle scroll-triggered motion instead of long blocks of explanatory text. Users decide whether to trust a brand in the first few seconds — a well-timed reveal does more work than a paragraph ever could.

"If your homepage needs three paragraphs to explain what you do, your design isn't doing its job."

2. Light, restrained palettes are winning

Dark, moody hero sections had their moment. What's converting now is closer to what you're reading this on — off-white backgrounds, a single accent color, and generous whitespace. It reads as confident rather than trying too hard.

  • Fewer than 3 colors in the core palette
  • One serif display font paired with one clean sans-serif
  • Whitespace treated as a design decision, not empty space

3. Pricing pages are getting simpler

The teardown of the week: a client-facing pricing page that cut its plan count from five to three and saw a meaningful lift in inquiries. More options rarely means more conversions — it usually just means more hesitation.
